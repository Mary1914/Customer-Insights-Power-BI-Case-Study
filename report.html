Customer Insights – Power BI Case Study

This report explores customer churn drivers using contract type, payment method, usage patterns, service calls, and extra charges.

Business Questions

  • Which customer segments churn the most?
  • How do contract type and payment method affect churn?
  • Does usage behavior or extra charges increase churn risk?
  • Are there geographic churn patterns?

Key Insights

1. Contract Type is a Strong Churn Driver

  • Monthly contract customers show significantly higher churn than yearly customers.
  • Longer account length correlates with lower churn.

2. Unlimited Data Plans Reduce Churn

  • Customers without unlimited plans churn more, especially at low (<5GB) usage levels.
  • Unlimited plans stabilize churn across all usage groups.

3. Extra Charges Increase Churn Risk

  • Higher extra international and data charges are linked to higher churn.
  • Customers facing bill surprises are more likely to leave.

4. Customer Service Calls Are a Warning Signal

  • Churned customers make significantly more service calls.
  • Repeated calls indicate unresolved issues.

5. Geographic Patterns Exist

  • Some states show higher churn clusters, suggesting regional service or pricing differences.

Results

  • Churn is highest among monthly, non-unlimited plan customers.
  • Extra charges and frequent support calls strongly correlate with churn.
  • Long-term contracts and unlimited plans improve retention.

Recommendations

  • Incentivize migration from monthly to yearly contracts.
  • Promote unlimited data plans to low-usage customers.
  • Reduce extra charges or improve transparency in billing.
  • Proactively target customers with high service call volumes.
  • Investigate high-churn states for operational improvements.

Conclusion

This Power BI dashboard demonstrates how customer behavior, pricing, and service quality directly impact churn. By combining KPIs, drill-down visuals, and geographic analysis, the report enables business leaders to identify at-risk customers early and apply targeted retention strategies.
